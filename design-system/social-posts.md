# social.plus Social Media Guidelines

> **Precedence note:** For social media tasks, the platform-specific tone, format, and copy structure in this file override the general `tone.md` rules. `terminology.md` still applies everywhere — it is never overridden.

Read this file before creating any social media content for social.plus.

Also read: `tone.md`, `colors.md`

---

## General Principles

Social media is where social.plus's personality shines most directly. Every post
should feel like it comes from a smart, confident team that genuinely loves what
they're building — not a marketing department.

**Goals by platform:**
- LinkedIn: Thought leadership, product announcements, team culture, developer community
- Instagram: Brand aesthetic, product visuals, community moments, culture
- Twitter/X: Real-time engagement, product updates, opinions, conversations

---

## LinkedIn

### Format
- **Text posts:** 1,200–1,500 characters for best engagement. Front-load with a strong
  hook in the first 2 lines (visible before "see more").
- **Image posts:** 1,200×628px (landscape) or 1080×1080px (square). Use brand colours
  and Figtree font for any text overlays.
- **Video posts:** Keep under 90 seconds for organic reach.
- **Articles:** For long-form thought leadership. 800–1,500 words.

### Copy structure
Line 1 (the hook): One strong sentence. A bold statement, a question, a surprising fact.
[blank line]
Lines 2–5: The substance. What you're sharing and why it matters.
[blank line]
CTA or closing thought: What should the reader do or think next?
[blank line]
Hashtags: 3–5 relevant hashtags at the end. Not in the body copy.

### Example
```
We just hit 10,000 communities powered by social.plus.

Three years ago, we were five people in a room convinced that community was
going to be the moat for every serious consumer product.

Turns out, we were right.

Here's what we've learned building infrastructure for the world's fastest-growing
community-led products 👇

[content continues...]

#community #socialplatform #growth #buildinpublic
```

### Tone for LinkedIn
- Professional but never stiff
- Share genuine opinions and learnings — not just announcements
- Engage with comments promptly
- Credit team members by name when appropriate

## Instagram

### Image specifications
- Square: 1080×1080px
- Portrait: 1080×1350px (preferred for feed)
- Stories/Reels: 1080×1920px

### Design for Instagram posts
- Use brand colours as backgrounds (dark `#111` works very well)
- Ultramarine (`#3B41EC`) or the Brand Blue gradient for accent areas
- Figtree Bold for any text overlays — minimum 48px in a 1080px frame
- Keep text to a minimum on images — let visuals lead, copy in caption
- Logo: use white version of the full name logo or icon in corner

### Caption structure
- Hook in first line (shown before "more")
- Keep emojis purposeful and on-brand — 1–3 per post max
- 3–10 hashtags, placed at the end or in first comment
- Tag relevant accounts when appropriate

### Content pillars for Instagram
1. **Product moments** — screenshots, UI highlights, feature showcases
2. **Community stories** — spotlight customers and communities built on social.plus
3. **Team & culture** — humans behind the product (authentic, not polished)
4. **Thought leadership** — visual takes on trends in community, social, and comms

### Reels / short video
- Hook in first 3 seconds
- Captions/subtitles always (most watched on mute)
- 15–30 seconds is the sweet spot for product demos
- Music should be upbeat but not distracting — check licensing

## Twitter / X

### Character limits
- Tweet: 280 characters (25,000 for X Premium but keep organic posts concise)
- Alt text for images: up to 1,000 characters (always add for accessibility)

### Copy approach
- One strong idea per tweet
- Lead with the most interesting thing — no warm-up sentences
- Use line breaks to aid scannability
- Threads for longer thoughts: first tweet must stand alone as a hook
- Reply promptly to mentions — Twitter/X rewards engagement

### Tweet types that perform well
1. **Opinionated takes:** "Hot take: [thing everyone in the industry is doing wrong]"
2. **Behind-the-scenes:** "We shipped [X] this week. Here's what it took."
3. **Community proof:** "A team at [Customer] just [achieved result] using social.plus."
4. **Useful resources:** "Quick thread on [topic] for anyone building [thing] 👇"
5. **Questions:** Genuine questions that invite the community to respond

### Tone for Twitter/X
- More casual than LinkedIn
- Wit is welcome — but punch lines must land, not fall flat
- Avoid performative positivity ("So excited to announce...")
- Be willing to have opinions. Fence-sitting gets ignored.

## Visual Assets — Quick Reference

When generating images for social:

**Backgrounds:** Brand Black `#111` (most versatile), Dark Navy `#27265E` (premium feel)
**Accents:** Ultramarine `#3B41EC`, Brand Blue Gradient, Brand Pink Gradient
**Text:** White `#FFFFFF` on dark backgrounds, weight 700 for headlines
**Font:** Figtree — always. If unavailable use Inter or Helvetica Neue.
**Logo position:** Bottom left or top left corner, white version, with adequate clearspace
**Image size for feed:**  1080×1080px (square) or 1080×1350px (portrait)

---

## What to Avoid

- Stock photos that look generic or corporate
- Excessive text on images (breaks Instagram algorithm and looks cluttered)
- Trend-chasing content that doesn't relate to social.plus's actual product or community
- Passive voice or hedging language ("We are pleased to announce...")
- Posting the same content identically across all platforms — adapt tone and format
