# Tone of Voice

The voice of social.plus reflects a company defining its category.

We speak with confidence, clarity, and energy. Our tone combines visionary thinking with technical credibility.

## Category-defining

We do not position ourselves as an incremental tool.

Our language reflects leadership in in-app community infrastructure. We describe the future of digital products as something that is already unfolding, and we speak as a company building that future.

## Visionary and technical

We combine strategic perspective with practical depth.

We can articulate long-term shifts in how apps evolve, while also demonstrating clear understanding of architecture, integration, and product mechanics.

This balance allows us to resonate with both business and technical stakeholders.

## Structured and intentional

Our writing is medium-length and structured.

We prefer clarity over density. Arguments are built logically. Claims are supported by reasoning rather than hype.

We avoid unnecessary jargon and empty buzzwords.

## Platform voice

We communicate as infrastructure.

The tone is not personal, casual, or founder-centric. It reflects a stable, scalable system that companies can rely on.

## Bold challenger

We are comfortable challenging outdated models.

We can speak directly about the limitations of rented platforms and the risk of remaining purely transactional.

Provocation should be intelligent and controlled, never emotional or dismissive.

## Energy level

Our communication carries high energy and forward momentum.

We believe in what we are building, and that conviction is visible in our language.

Confidence should never cross into exaggeration. Authority comes from clarity and precision.

# Writing Style Rules

The following rules apply to all written communication produced by social.plus, including website copy, sales materials, product documentation, investor messaging, and AI-generated content.

These rules exist to protect clarity, credibility, and consistency.

## Structural guidelines

- Use structured, medium-length paragraphs.
- Avoid overly short, fragmented writing.
- Build arguments logically and progressively.
- Use headings to create hierarchy and clarity.
- Bullet points are allowed when they improve clarity.
- Do not use emojis in public-facing communication.

## Sentence construction

- Use active voice.
- Avoid unnecessary complexity.
- Avoid rhetorical fluff and filler phrases.
- Do not use em dashes.
- Keep sentences precise and intentional.

## Tone discipline

- Avoid exaggeration.
- Avoid empty superlatives unless supported by reasoning.
- Avoid dramatic or emotional phrasing.

Confidence should come from clarity, not hype.

## Claims and proof

The following are strictly prohibited:

- Inventing statistics.
- Inventing customer quotes.
- Inventing customer names.
- Claiming features not documented in official materials.
- Implying guaranteed business outcomes.

If a claim cannot be verified, it should not be stated.

## Precision over decoration

We prioritize clarity over cleverness.

Language should communicate value directly rather than attempting to impress through style alone.
