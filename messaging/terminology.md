# Approved Terminology

Consistency in language protects positioning and clarity.

The following terms should be used consistently across all communication, both internal and external.

## Company name

Always write the brand as:

social.plus

Do not capitalize it unless required by grammar at the beginning of a sentence.

Do not add descriptors such as "formerly" or alternate spellings.

## Product components

Use the following names exactly as written:

- APIs
- SDKs
- UIKit (when referring to technical implementation components)
- UI Kit (when referring to Figma or design assets)
- Console
- Portal
- Dashboard

Avoid inventing alternative names or shorthand variations.

## How we refer to customers

Preferred terms:

- Brands
- Apps

Use "organizations" only when broader corporate context is required.

## How we refer to end users

Preferred term:

- Users

Avoid switching between members, participants, customers, or other synonyms unless context specifically requires it.

## What we provide

Preferred primary term:

- Infrastructure

Acceptable supporting terms when needed:

- Platform
- System
- Layer

Infrastructure should remain the dominant descriptor.

## Community language

Use:

- Community
- Social layer

Select based on context. "In-app community" is a preferred and encouraged term.

Avoid referring to social.plus as a social network.

## Pillar references

Engage, Analyze, and Monetize can be used when helpful, but they do not need to appear in every communication.

They are a narrative structure, not mandatory vocabulary.

# Forbidden and Risky Terminology

Precision in language protects credibility and prevents misalignment between expectation and reality.

The following guidelines exist to reduce positioning drift and overstatement.

## Terms to avoid

Do not describe social.plus as:

- A social network
- A forum platform
- A chat tool
- An ad network

These labels narrow the perception of the platform and misrepresent the scope of the infrastructure.

## Growth guarantees

Avoid absolute claims such as:

- Guaranteed growth
- Guaranteed retention
- Guaranteed revenue

Outcomes depend on implementation quality, use case design, and operational commitment from the customer.

## "Plug and play" usage

The phrase "plug and play" may be used carefully in technical contexts when speaking to developers, particularly when referring to SDK integration and rapid MVP builds.

It should not be used in business-facing messaging in ways that imply effortless community success.

Community infrastructure can be integrated efficiently, but sustained engagement requires strategy and ongoing effort.

## "Easy community" language

Avoid framing community building as simple or automatic.

It is acceptable to highlight how social.plus reduces technical complexity, but messaging must reinforce that meaningful participation is built over time.

## Overly narrow positioning

Avoid language that reduces social.plus to a single capability or feature category.

The platform should consistently be framed as infrastructure rather than a standalone feature.
